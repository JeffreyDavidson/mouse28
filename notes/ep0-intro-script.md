# Episode 0: Welcome to Mouse28
**Format:** Conversational with talking points | **Target:** 5-10 minutes

---

## COLD OPEN (30 seconds)

**Jeffrey:** "So... how many times have you been to Disney World?"
**Cassie:** [answer]
**Jeffrey:** "We go every single week. And no, we're not exaggerating. Welcome to Mouse28."

*[Intro music/jingle — 5-10 seconds]*

---

## SEGMENT 1: WHO WE ARE (2-3 minutes)

**Talking points to hit:**
- We're Jeffrey and Cassie, a married couple living in central Florida
- We visit Disney parks pretty much every week
- We're not travel agents, influencers, or Disney employees — just a family that genuinely loves this place
- The name "Mouse28" — a nod to 1928, when Mickey Mouse debuted in *Steamboat Willie*
- We started this because we kept getting the same questions from friends and family: "You go AGAIN?" and "How do you do Disney so often?"

**Natural conversation starter:**
> Jeffrey: "People always ask us — don't you get bored?" / Cassie: [react naturally — this should get a genuine laugh or response]

---

## SEGMENT 2: WHAT THIS PODCAST IS ABOUT (2-3 minutes)

**Talking points to hit:**
- This isn't a Disney news podcast (there are plenty of great ones already)
- We talk about the *experience* — what it's actually like being at the parks week after week
- Topics we'll cover:
  - **Park tips** — things you only learn by going constantly (best times, hidden spots, underrated rides)
  - **Food** — honest reviews, not sponsored ones. What's actually worth $18.
  - **Accessibility** — Disney through the lens of families who need extra support (more on this in a future episode)
  - **Disney+, merchandise, resort stays** — the full Disney ecosystem
  - **Real talk** — what Disney gets right, what they get wrong, and what we wish they'd change

**Natural conversation starter:**
> Jeffrey: "What's the one thing you wish someone had told you before your first Disney trip?" / Cassie: [answer — this previews the kind of practical value the show delivers]

---

## SEGMENT 3: WHAT TO EXPECT (1-2 minutes)

**Talking points to hit:**
- Episodes will be a mix of planned topics and whatever happened at the parks that week
- We'll have some solo episodes, some together, maybe guests down the road
- Keeping it real — we love Disney but we're not going to pretend everything is magical all the time
- We have a blog at **mouse28.com** where we go deeper on guides, reviews, and resources

---

## SEGMENT 4: CLOSE + CALL TO ACTION (1 minute)

**Talking points to hit:**
- "Next episode, we're going to share our personal story — why Disney became such a big part of our family's life."
  *(Teases the Viola episode without spoiling it)*
- Where to find us:
  - **mouse28.com** — blog posts, park guides, resources
  - Follow/subscribe on [Apple Podcasts / Spotify / YouTube — wherever you're listening]
  - Social media handles (if ready)
- Sign off: something natural, not scripted. Maybe a running joke or catchphrase you develop over time.

**Suggested close:**
> Jeffrey: "Thanks for being here for episode zero. We'll see you at the parks." / Cassie: "Literally. We'll be there."

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES
- **Tone:** Like you're explaining the podcast to a friend at dinner — warm, casual, a little funny
- **Pacing:** Don't rush. Pauses and laughter are good. 5 minutes of genuine conversation > 10 minutes of hitting every point.
- **Music:** Light background music under intro/outro. Nothing during conversation.
- **Edit:** Minimal. Keep it natural. Cut dead air and stumbles, leave personality.
